Nutrition Basics

Healthy food isn't just about filling your stomach; it's the best way to energize your body and protect it from diseases.

Introduction: Why is Nutrition Important?

Nutrition means giving our bodies the right food in the right amounts. Just like a car needs fuel to run, our bodies need nutrients to function, grow, and stay healthy. Good nutrition gives us energy, strengthens our immune system, and protects us from many chronic diseases.

Macronutrients

Macronutrients are the nutrients our bodies need in large amounts. There are three types:

1. Carbohydrates (The Main Source of Energy)

  • Function: They are the main source of energy for our body and brain.
  • Good Sources (Complex Carbs): Whole grains (wheat, millet, oats), legumes, fruits, and vegetables. They provide slow-release energy and are rich in fiber.
  • Sources to Avoid (Simple Carbs): Sugar, refined flour, white bread. They provide instant energy but are not good for health.

2. Protein (The Body's Building Blocks)

  • Function: Protein is essential for building and repairing muscles, bones, skin, and hair.
  • Sources: Lentils, chickpeas, kidney beans, paneer, tofu, eggs, fish, and lean meat.

3. Fats (Healthy Fats are Essential)

  • Function: They are important for brain health, hormone production, and absorbing vitamins.
  • Good Sources (Unsaturated Fats): Nuts (almonds, walnuts), seeds (flax, chia), avocado, and olive oil.
  • Sources to Avoid (Bad Fats): Trans fats and saturated fats found in fried foods, packaged snacks, and bakery items.

Micronutrients and Water

Vitamins, Minerals, and Water

  • Vitamins and Minerals: The body needs these in small amounts, but they are crucial for immunity, bone health, and energy production. The best way to get them is to "eat the rainbow" ‚Äì meaning, eat fruits and vegetables of all colors.
  • Water: Water is essential for regulating body temperature, aiding digestion, and transporting nutrients to every part of the body. Drink at least 8-10 glasses of water a day.

The Principle of a Balanced Plate

The easiest way to create a healthy meal is to follow the concept of a "balanced plate":

  • Half Plate (50%): Fill with vegetables and salad.
  • Quarter Plate (25%): Fill with a protein source (like lentils, paneer).
  • Quarter Plate (25%): Fill with complex carbohydrates (like roti, brown rice).

Easy Tips for Healthy Eating

  • Eat 5 types of fruits/veg daily
  • Avoid processed foods
  • Drink plenty of water
  • Reduce sugar and salt
  • Eat home-cooked meals
  • Fill your plate with colors
